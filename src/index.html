Physics PhD Student at Clemson University

  I am a current physics Ph.D. student at Clemson University. My current area of research is AMO(Atomic Molecular and Optical) physics. I hold a B.S. in Physics from Bridgewater College in which I also earned two minors in computer science and mathematics. When I am not performing research or working on programming projects, I enjoy golf, hiking, and playing video games.

  I love problem solving and I believe that is why I am so drawn to physics and programming, because they not only allow me to learn, but they also challenge me. To me, there is no better feeling than seeing a project I've been working on for hours/days/weeks/etc. finally come to fruition.

  I believe that one of my biggest character strengths is leadership. When I was younger I spent a lot of time at my local First Tee program and that is where I was able to learn about what it means to be a good leader. I was even accepted to a leadership academy across the country when I was 13. The skills I learned there where able to help me later to become the chapter president of my high schools TSA team and I was the vice-president of the physics club at Bridgewater College.

  Overall, I enjoy always learning more and am always looking for opportunities to expand and improve my current knowledge and skills.
